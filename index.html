Abstract

Recent advancements in deep learning have led to significant progress in analyzing, controlling, and generating audio speech. While various approaches have emerged to tackle these tasks individually, this article focuses on a novel generative self-supervised learning method called AnCoGen, based on masked autoencoders (MAE). While MAE-based methods have shown promise in tasks like classification and segmentation, their potential for controllability and interpretability still needs to be explored. This paper introduces the AnCoGen approach, which facilitates the analysis of audio speech by estimating key factors of variation such as pitch, content, noise level, loudness, and identity. Moreover, it enables the generation of audio speech from these factors and provides control over the synthesized speech by modifying these factors. Extensive experiments are carried out to study the capabilities of AnCoGen to analyze, control and generate speech audio.

Analysis-generation
We first present the results of an analysis-generation process on audio speech data. The analysis involves encoding audio speech sequences (\(\mathbf{x}^{(a)}\) ) and decoding the factors of variation (\(\mathbf{x}^{(f)}\) ). Subsequently, the generation step consists of generating the audio sequence (\(\mathbf{x}^{(a)}\) ) from these factors obtained without any modification, intending to reconstruct the input sequence faithfully.

Analysis-control-generation
1- Speech enhancement
How to do speech enhancement with AnCoGen? for that, we perform an analysis-control-generation process on noisy audio speech data. The analysis involves encoding noised audio speech sequences (\(\mathbf{x}^{(a)}\) ) and decoding the factors of variation (\(\mathbf{x}^{(f)}\) ). Subsequently, the control step consists of controlling and manipulating the SNR and C50. Concretely, we increase the value of these two factors of variation by a value denoted respectively by SNR control and C50 control. Lastly, the generation step synthesizes the audio sequence (\(\mathbf{x}^{(a)}\) ) from these factors modified in order to denoise the audio speech signal.

2- Voice Conversion
AnCoGen functions as a voice conversion tool via the analysis-control-generation process between source signals and target signals. In the control step, we switch only the identity information from the target to the source signal, all other factors of variation of the source are preserved.
